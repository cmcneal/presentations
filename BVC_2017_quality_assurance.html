So, what is QA?

Executing manual, pre-defined steps from pre-determined lists of scripted checks that will test all possibilities, inputs, and outcomes to make sure that there are no bugs in the software.

Good luck with that! If you can't predict all of the places that the bugs will be beforehand, why not help prevent them from happening in the first place?

Quality assurance helps to protect the reputation of a company, something that is hard to recover.

QA is

  • Asking questions, not just about software functionality
  • Working on the whole process, inception to delivery
  • Helping to prevent issues before they happen
  • Helping to keep things working as expected
  • A part of the team, enabling the team's success
  • Everyone's responsibility

QA is not

  • Just testing
  • Criticizing or attacking
  • Blocking progress
  • Eliminating all bugs
  • Preventing failure

Nor is it

..always easy
Can't see the forest for the trees
..for everyone
..an assurance of success
..an after thought
..quality control

An option!

Quality control tends to be more of an assembly line, after-the-fact check. Quality assurance should be more focused on the whole picture, and prevention.

QA vs. Tester

What's the difference?

  • Testers test
  • QA asks questions
  • Testers ensure completeness
  • QA enables success
  • Testers look at requirements
  • QA looks at team success
  • Testers can be seen as a roadblocks
  • QA can speed up delivery
